OVERVIEW

Challenge

COVID 19 has utterly changed how people interact with each other. Social isolation, fear of unknown, unhelpful comments online all contributed to our increased level of anxiety. Social interaction at times as such is in jeopardy.

How do we facilitate and redefine human interactions within the constraints of social distancing during global pandemic?

Due to social restrictions, this project was built completely online as a digital simulated programming model to simulate a real-life experience.

Concept video

EMPATHISE

User research

In order to understand the situation and experiences users have during COVID19, I conducted a survey with 29 users and interviewed 2 users via Zoom.

Key findings
  • Natural places are where people seek for the peace of mind.
  • Internet, particularly social media, during a global pandemic can become somewhere people lash their anger and frustration.
  • People long for positivity and mutual support.

↑ KONG MING LANTERN

Kong Ming Lantern

In Asian cultures, Kong Ming Lantern represents hope. When being released to the sky, wishful messages are thought to be received by the universe.

This design has borrowed the concept of Kong Ming Lantern. An interactive digital river lantern installation was created to provide a space during this pandemic where people can make wish for the future.

CONCEPTUALISE & PROTOTYPE

Stage 1

A social media campaign introduces this event to local community. People in local communities and general public are invited to post wishful comments and positive messages on social media with #lightthelantern to stand in solidarity with the world that suffers during COVID19.

↑ STORYBOARD

Stage 2

Week of Hope commences during week one of June 2020. Digital lanterns are displayed afloat on Parramatta River. Lanterns are made with translucent water- proof material. The frontside of each lantern acts as a LED screen. Each lantern also has an infrared sensor that detects movements and a built-in speaker. When the infrared sensor detects an object nearby, lantern will be switched from dim to bright and the messages people posted on social media will start scrolling up on lantern screen. Exotic and calm melody starts to play.

Key features of the interactive digital lantern
  • Digital lantern displays messages in a rotating way.
  • Simulate real lanterns that have shimmering candle light effects.
  • When user interacts, lantern lights up and music starts playing. Lantern goes dim and music stops when user stops interaction.

Due to COVID19, producing physical products became impossible. Therefore, the focus was to create an interactive online experience to simulate the real installation.
